Guides

Atlantic City offers a unique blend of excitement and relaxation, with its lively Boardwalk serving as a central hub for entertainment. From top-notch dining and bustling nightlife to family-friendly activities and budget-friendly options, there’s something for everyone in this iconic city.

These guides will help you navigate the best restaurants, nightspots, and attractions, and provide tips on how to enjoy your visit while keeping costs in check. Whether you’re planning a family trip, a romantic escape, or a cost-conscious adventure, you’ll find valuable insights to enhance your Atlantic City experience.

Food in Atlantic City: A Culinary Exploration

Atlantic City is a vibrant destination that offers a culinary scene as diverse and exciting as its entertainment options. Whether you’re a gourmet aficionado or simply looking for a great meal, the city has something for everyone.

High-End Dining

For a truly memorable dining experience, Atlantic City boasts several upscale restaurants that deliver on both flavor and atmosphere.

Chef Vola’s is a beloved institution in Atlantic City, known for its classic Italian cuisine and intimate ambiance. This hidden gem is renowned for its traditional recipes, generous portions, and exceptional service. Reservations are highly recommended as the restaurant is small and often fully booked.

The Palm Atlantic City offers a quintessential steakhouse experience with a focus on top-quality meats and seafood. Located in the heart of the city, The Palm combines elegant décor with a welcoming atmosphere, making it perfect for a special night out or a celebratory meal.

Casual Eats

For a more laid-back dining experience, Atlantic City features a number of casual yet highly satisfying eateries.

White House Sub Shop is a local legend known for its overstuffed subs and classic Italian-American fare. The shop’s generous portions and hearty sandwiches have made it a favorite among both locals and visitors. It’s the perfect spot for a quick, delicious lunch or a casual dinner.

Dock’s Oyster House has been serving fresh seafood for over a century. With its rich history and commitment to quality, Dock’s offers a variety of seafood dishes, including their famous clam chowder and oysters on the half shell. The restaurant’s warm, inviting atmosphere complements its delectable menu.

International Flavors

Atlantic City’s diverse dining scene also includes a variety of international cuisines.

Amada, created by renowned chef José Garces, brings a taste of Spain to the city with its selection of tapas and contemporary Spanish dishes. The restaurant’s chic design and flavorful small plates make it a fantastic choice for a sophisticated meal.

China House offers authentic Chinese cuisine in a more relaxed setting. Known for its flavorful dishes and generous portions, it’s a great place to enjoy traditional Chinese favorites like Peking duck or dumplings.

Sweet Treats

No visit to Atlantic City is complete without indulging in some sweet treats.

Hard Rock CaféHard Rock Café not only serves classic American fare but also offers a range of tempting desserts. From their signature brownie sundae to a rich chocolate cake, the Hard Rock Café provides a sweet ending to any meal.

A stroll along the Atlantic City Boardwalk is the perfect way to sample a variety of boardwalk treats. Try the classic saltwater taffy, enjoy a warm funnel cake, or grab a cone of creamy ice cream from one of the many vendors.

Brunch Spots

For a leisurely brunch, Atlantic City offers several delightful options.

Nola’s provides a cozy atmosphere and a menu filled with brunch favorites like eggs Benedict and French toast. The restaurant’s charming décor and delicious food make it a great spot for a relaxed brunch with friends or family.

The Iron Room features a brunch menu that emphasizes local ingredients and inventive dishes. Enjoy a range of options from classic pancakes to more adventurous fare, all in a sophisticated setting.

Nightlife in Atlantic City: Where the Fun Never Stops

Atlantic City is renowned for its lively nightlife scene, offering something for everyone, from high-energy clubs to cozy bars. Here’s a guide to experiencing the best of the city after dark.

Casinos

Atlantic City’s casinos are not just for gambling; they also serve as vibrant nightlife hubs.

Borgata’s Premier Nightclub is a top destination for those looking to dance the night away. With its state-of-the-art sound system and impressive lineup of DJs, the nightclub creates a high-energy atmosphere that attracts both locals and visitors.

Ocean Casino Resort’s HQ2 is a stylish beach club and nightlife venue that offers stunning ocean views and a lively party scene. Its combination of great music, beautiful setting, and upscale amenities make it a must-visit spot for nightlife enthusiasts.

Bars and Lounges

If you prefer a more relaxed evening, Atlantic City has numerous bars and lounges where you can unwind.

The Irish Pub is a beloved local spot known for its friendly atmosphere and extensive beer selection. The pub’s casual vibe and hearty pub fare make it a great place to kick back with a drink and enjoy some conversation.

The Raging Bull offers a laid-back environment with a focus on cocktails and casual drinks. It’s a perfect venue for a low-key evening or a pre-party gathering with friends.

Live Music and Entertainment

Atlantic City’s nightlife also includes a range of live entertainment options.

The Sound Waves at Hard Rock is a premier venue for live music, featuring performances by top artists across various genres. The venue’s acoustics and intimate setting make for an unforgettable music experience.

The Comedy Stop at Tropicana offers a night of laughter with its lineup of stand-up comedians. The comedy club provides a fun, relaxed atmosphere where you can enjoy a good laugh with friends or loved ones.

Dancing

For those who love to dance, Atlantic City’s nightlife scene includes several hot spots

Dusk Nightclub at Caesars is renowned for its vibrant atmosphere and themed events. With its impressive décor and high-energy music, it’s the perfect place for a night of dancing and socializing.

The Pool After Dark transforms an indoor pool into a lively nightclub with regular DJ performances and themed parties. Its unique setting and fun vibe make it a standout choice for an exciting night out.

Cocktail Lounges

For a more sophisticated night out, check out Atlantic City’s cocktail lounges.

The Bar at The Water Club offers an elegant setting with stunning views of the marina. The bar’s expertly crafted cocktails and serene ambiance make it an ideal spot for a refined evening.

VUE Rooftop Bar provides panoramic city views and a chic atmosphere. Enjoy a sunset cocktail or a nightcap while taking in the beautiful skyline.

Tours and Sightseeing in Atlantic City: Discover the City’s Highlights

Atlantic City is more than just casinos and nightlife; it also offers a range of tours and sightseeing opportunities that showcase its unique character and attractions.

Boardwalk and Beach

The Atlantic City Boardwalk is an iconic attraction that stretches for miles along the coast. This bustling promenade is lined with shops, eateries, and attractions, offering something for everyone. Whether you’re taking a leisurely stroll, renting a bike, or simply people-watching, the Boardwalk is a quintessential part of the Atlantic City experience.

Steel Pier , located right on the Boardwalk, is a historic amusement park that has been entertaining visitors for decades. With its classic rides, games, and family-friendly attractions, it’s a great place to enjoy some nostalgic fun.

Historic and Cultural Sites

Atlantic City’s rich history and cultural heritage are showcased at several notable sites.

Absecon Lighthouse is one of the tallest lighthouses in the U.S. and offers breathtaking views from the top. Visitors can climb the 228 steps to the lantern room and enjoy panoramic views of the city and coastline. The lighthouse also features a museum with exhibits on maritime history.

Atlantic City Historical Museum provides a deeper understanding of the city’s past. The museum’s exhibits cover a range of topics, including the city’s development, famous landmarks, and notable figures. It’s a great place to learn about the history that has shaped Atlantic City.

Nature and Parks

For a change of pace, Atlantic City’s surrounding nature reserves and parks offer a tranquil escape.

Cape May County Park & Zoo is a short drive from Atlantic City and provides a free, family-friendly outing. The zoo features a variety of animals, including lions, giraffes, and bears, set amidst beautiful parkland.

Leeds Pointis a nearby nature reserve known for its peaceful walking trails and opportunities to spot local wildlife. It’s a great spot for a relaxing nature walk and a chance to enjoy the area’s natural beauty.

Boat Tours

Exploring Atlantic City from the water offers a unique perspective on the city’s skyline and coastline.

The AC Boat Tour provides scenic views of the city’s landmarks and waterfront. The tour offers informative commentary and a relaxing way to take in the sights.

Cruise to Nowhere is a short cruise that combines live entertainment with ocean views. It’s a fun option for those looking to enjoy a night out on the water without leaving the area.

Landmarks

No visit to Atlantic City is complete without seeing some of its landmark attractions.

Boardwalk Hall is an architectural marvel known for its historic pipe organ and grand design. The building hosts a variety of events and performances, and its stunning architecture makes it worth a visit.

The Quarter at Tropicana is a vibrant shopping and entertainment area that replicates the charm of New Orleans. With its eclectic mix of shops, restaurants, and entertainment options, it’s a great place to spend an afternoon or evening.

Family Fun in Atlantic City: Activities for All Ages

Atlantic City isn’t just for adults; it offers a variety of family-friendly activities that make it a great destination for all ages. Here’s a guide to some of the best family-oriented attractions and activities in the city.

1. Steel Pier Amusement Park

Located on the Boardwalk, Steel Pier is a classic family destination. The amusement park has been a staple of Atlantic City since 1898 and continues to offer a range of fun rides and games. Families can enjoy everything from a carousel and bumper cars to thrilling rides like the Ferris wheel and roller coasters. The park also features arcades and food stands, making it a full day of fun for the whole family.

2. Atlantic City Aquarium

The Atlantic City Aquarium provides an educational and entertaining experience for children and adults alike. Located in the Historic Gardner's Basin, the aquarium showcases a variety of marine life, including fish, sharks, and stingrays. Interactive exhibits and touch tanks allow kids to get up close with sea creatures, and educational programs provide a fun way to learn about marine biology.

3. Cape May County Park & Zoo

For a change of pace, Atlantic City’s surrounding nature reserves and parks offer a tranquil escape.

A short drive from Atlantic City, the Cape May County Park & Zoo is a fantastic destination for families. The zoo is home to a diverse range of animals, including lions, giraffes, and bears, and offers plenty of space for picnicking and exploring. Admission to the zoo is free, although donations are encouraged. The park also features playgrounds and walking trails, making it a great spot for a day out in nature.

4. Atlantic City Historical Museum

For families interested in learning about the city’s past, the Atlantic City Historical Museum provides an engaging experience. The museum offers exhibits on the city's history, including its development, landmarks, and famous personalities. Interactive displays and artifacts make the history accessible and interesting for children and adults.

5. Boardwalk Activities

In addition to its amusement park, the Atlantic City Boardwalk offers a variety of family-friendly activities. Kids can enjoy bike rentals, explore the shops, or take a ride on one of the many small amusement rides scattered along the Boardwalk. Don’t miss the chance to try classic Boardwalk treats like saltwater taffy or funnel cakes.

Romantic Getaways in Atlantic City: Perfect Escapes for Couples

Atlantic City offers several romantic experiences that are perfect for couples looking for a getaway. Whether you’re celebrating an anniversary or just enjoying a weekend together, these ideas will help you create lasting memories.

1. Romantic Dinner Spots

The Palm Atlantic City provides an elegant dining experience with its refined atmosphere and exceptional steak and seafood offerings. The upscale setting is perfect for a romantic dinner.

Chef Vola’s is another excellent choice for a special evening. Its intimate, cozy atmosphere combined with delicious Italian cuisine makes it ideal for a romantic meal.

2. Beachfront Strolls

Take a leisurely walk along the Atlantic City Beach at sunset for a romantic evening. The peaceful ambiance and beautiful ocean views create a perfect setting for a quiet, intimate stroll. For a more active experience, you can rent bikes and ride along the Boardwalk.

3. Spa Retreats

Indulge in a couples’ spa day at Borgata Hotel Casino & Spa. The spa offers a range of treatments, including massages, facials, and relaxation therapies. Enjoy a day of pampering together in a luxurious setting.

4. Scenic Cruises

A Cruise to Nowhere provides a unique opportunity for a romantic evening on the water. With live entertainment and beautiful ocean views, it’s an excellent way to enjoy a night out together.

5. Boardwalk Entertainment

Spend a fun evening exploring the Boardwalk together. Watch a show at Boardwalk hall, enjoy a romantic dinner at one of the Boardwalk restaurants, and take in the lively atmosphere. The Boardwalk also has charming shops where you can pick up a souvenir to remember your trip.

6. Rooftop Bars

VUE Rooftop Bar offers stunning panoramic views of the city and ocean, making it a perfect spot for a romantic cocktail. Enjoy the sunset with your loved one while sipping on expertly crafted drinks.

Budget-Friendly Atlantic City: Enjoying the City Without Breaking the Bank

Atlantic City can be a fantastic destination even if you’re traveling on a budget. With a little planning, you can enjoy the city’s attractions and activities without overspending. Here’s how to have a great time without breaking the bank.

1. Affordable Dining Options

White House Sub Shop offers delicious and hearty subs at a reasonable price. It’s a great spot for a quick and satisfying meal without spending a lot.

Tony’s Baltimore Grill is another budget-friendly option known for its pizza and Italian dishes. The casual atmosphere and affordable prices make it a popular choice for a relaxed meal.

2. Free and Low-Cost Attractions

Atlantic City Beach is free to access and offers a great place to relax, sunbathe, or take a swim. Bring your own beach gear to avoid rental costs.

The Atlantic City Boardwalk

is another free attraction that provides a great place to walk, people-watch, and enjoy the sights. Many of the Boardwalk’s activities and shops are also budget-friendly.

3. Discounted Entertainment

Check out Boardwalk Hall for free or low-cost events. The venue hosts a variety of performances and shows, and sometimes offers discounted tickets for certain events.

5. Boardwalk Entertainment

Spend a fun evening exploring the Boardwalk together. Watch a show at Boardwalk hall, enjoy a romantic dinner at one of the Boardwalk restaurants, and take in the lively atmosphere. The Boardwalk also has charming shops where you can pick up a souvenir to remember your trip

The Quarter at Tropicana often features free live entertainment, including music and street performers. Explore the area for free events and lively street scenes.

4. Budget-Friendly Attractions

Visit the Atlantic City Historical Museum for a small admission fee or donation. It offers a fascinating look at the city’s history and is an inexpensive way to spend an afternoon.

Cape May County Park & Zoo is a great option for a budget-friendly day trip. The zoo is free to enter, although donations are appreciated. It’s a wonderful place to spend time outdoors and see a variety of animals.

5. Affordable Shopping

The Outlets at The Walk provides a range of discount shopping options. You can find deals on clothing, accessories, and more, making it a great place to pick up bargains and souvenirs.

6. Use Public Transportation

Save on transportation costs by using the Jitney service or NJ Transit. These options are more affordable than taxis or rideshare services and provide easy access to key attractions
